Cells

Eukaryotes vs. Prokaryotes

Cells can be separated into two groups- eukaryotes and prokaryotes. The key difference between eukaryotes and prokaryotes is that eukaryotes have nuclei and prokaryotes do not. Eukaryotes also tend to be larger than prokaryotes, because they contain nuclei and oftentimes, other organelles. Examples of Eukaryotes include: Human cells, amoebas, neurons, etc. Most bacteria are prokaryotes.

Plant cells vs. Animal cells

Plant cells and animal cells are visibly different. Plant cells contain certain organelles that animal cells do not have. One of the more obvious organelles that they have is the cell wall. The cell wall surrounds the cytoplasm of the plant cell, protecting it. Plant cells also have chloroplasts (or chloroplastids,) that can convert sunlight into energy. Finally, plants also have large vacuoles, to store water.
